课题简介 Introduction

Cities need energy choices balancing cost, carbon, and ventilation. Rising heat and cooling demand increase emissions pressure; ventilation corridors help when siting respects airflow. Professional CFD/WRF tools are powerful but hard for learners—serious games offer immediate feedback and logged choices.

Mayor Climate Game is a browser prototype: allocate CNY 10M across coal, wind, and heat pumps on a 12×12 terrain grid with live temperature, wind, carbon, and composite score.

TakeawayCities must balance cheap energy, low carbon, and good airflow—but pro tools are too hard for beginners. This game makes those tradeoffs easy to see and play with.

研究意义 Significance

  • Makes tradeoffs visible without simulation infrastructure.
  • Auto-logs layout choices for small-scale classroom research.
  • Complements—not replaces—professional urban-climate models.

TakeawayThe game is a low-cost teaching tool: players learn by doing, and every choice is saved for later study.

研究问题 Research Question

How does the game shape facility choices and post-play understanding of budget–carbon–ventilation tradeoffs?

Hypothesis: Participants avoid coal on ventilation-sensitive cells and answer the ventilation rule correctly after one round.

TakeawayWe ask: after one play session, do people place facilities wisely and understand why ventilation matters?

研究方法 Methods

  • Pilot: N = 15 sessions (10 earlier + 5 pre/post TXT exports), July 2026.
  • Measures: Pre/post cognition (4) + attitude (2) + gameplay survey + score, carbon, placements.
  • Analysis: Descriptive coding from TXT exports and Supabase; no inferential tests.
  • Boundary: Rule-based heuristics, not CFD.

TakeawayFifteen session records coded (10 earlier + 5 pre/post exports). We counted scores, carbon, layouts, and survey answers—no advanced statistics.

研究及实践过程 Process

1Deploy web app (Vercel); 12×12 grid with corridor & outlet terrain.
2Volunteers play one round with live score & mission bar.
3Pre/post survey + TXT export; 15 sessions analyzed in total.
4Compare layouts; flag coal on outlets; track cognition pre vs post.

TakeawayConsent → pre-test → play → post-test → save data. Fifteen session records analyzed end-to-end.

研究结果 Results

15 sessions total: 10 earlier exports + 5 pre/post TXT reports (July 2026).

Data Analysis · 数据分析
  • Survey: 15/15 (100%) answered the ventilation question correctly; 11/15 said they chose coal to save budget.
  • Behavior: 0/15 placed coal on wind outlets.
  • Scores: Range 51–88 (mean ≈ 77). Carbon range 0–1440 t (mean ≈ 438 t).
  • Pattern: Low carbon (0–120 t, n=9) → scores mostly 84–88; high carbon (240–1440 t, n=6) → scores 51–81.
  • Pre/post (n=5): Cognition 3/4 before and after (no change in this subset).

TakeawayAll 15 session records show correct ventilation knowledge and no coal at outlets. Most (11/15) cited budget pressure—4/15 chose zero-coal layouts. Carbon spread: 0 t vs. 1440 t. Pre/post cognition unchanged at 3/4 (n=5).

结论 Conclusions

  1. Strong support: 15/15 showed accurate ventilation knowledge; 0/15 placed coal on outlets.
  2. Mixed: 11/15 cited budget pressure for coal; 4/15 chose zero-coal wind layouts.
  3. Carbon gap (0 vs. 1440 t) shows facility mix—not budget alone—drives emissions.
  4. Pre/post: 5 exports with matched scores—cognition stayed 3/4 (no gain in this subset).

Limitation: N=15 session records; exploratory pilot; simplified model—not confirmatory evidence.

TakeawayFifteen sessions show tradeoffs visibly: clean layouts score 84–88; an all-coal strategy scores 51 with 1440 t carbon.
