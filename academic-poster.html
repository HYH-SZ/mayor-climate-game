课题简介 Introduction

Cities need energy choices balancing cost, carbon, and ventilation. Rising heat and cooling demand increase emissions pressure; ventilation corridors help when siting respects airflow. Professional CFD/WRF tools are powerful but hard for learners—serious games offer immediate feedback and logged choices.

Mayor Climate Game is a browser prototype: allocate CNY 10M across coal, wind, and heat pumps on a 12×12 terrain grid with live temperature, wind, carbon, and composite score.

研究意义 Significance

  • Makes tradeoffs visible without simulation infrastructure.
  • Auto-logs layout choices for small-scale classroom research.
  • Complements—not replaces—professional urban-climate models.

研究问题 Research Question

How does the game shape facility choices and post-play understanding of budget–carbon–ventilation tradeoffs?

Hypothesis: Participants avoid coal on ventilation-sensitive cells and answer the ventilation rule correctly after one round.

研究方法 Methods

  • Pilot: N = 3 volunteers, July 2026, one round each.
  • Measures: 2 survey items (budget motivation; ventilation rule) + logged score, carbon (t CO₂e), placements.
  • Analysis: Descriptive coding from Supabase; no inferential tests.
  • Boundary: Rule-based heuristics, not CFD.

研究及实践过程 Process

1Deploy web app (Vercel); 12×12 grid with corridor & outlet terrain.
23 volunteers play one round with live score & mission bar.
3Post-play survey + TXT export; 3 sessions stored in Supabase.
4Compare 2 unique layouts; flag coal on outlets.

Limitation: N=3; exploratory pilot; simplified model—not confirmatory evidence.
