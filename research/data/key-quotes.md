# Key Quotes and Facts

## Cooling demand and urban heat

- IEA, 2023: Energy demand for space cooling has increased more than twice as fast as overall energy demand in buildings over the last decade.
- IEA, 2023: Rapid AC growth can increase grid stress, greenhouse gas emissions, local air pollution, power outages, urban heat island effects, and energy poverty.
- IEA, 2023: City and building design affect cooling demand; natural ventilation and urban green spaces are part of sustainable cooling, not just mechanical air conditioning.

## Urban ventilation corridors

- Beijing case: Urban ventilation corridors can use parks, green spaces, and prevailing wind directions to improve urban wind and heat environments.
- Guiyang case: Ventilation corridor planning can form first-, second-, and third-level air duct networks using green space, water systems, and road networks.
- Zhumadian case: RS, GIS, and CFD can identify potential wind corridors and locate building-layout barriers that weaken ventilation.

## Serious games and public learning

- Energy Game: A serious game can let students choose between energy options under cost constraints, while teaching energy supply tradeoffs.
- Chill City: Interactive heat-planning games can help non-expert players understand environmental, social, and economic tradeoffs of urban heat strategies.
- SETS: Role-play serious games can support local energy transition discussion by letting participants represent policymakers, energy developers, and civil society.

## How this informs the MVP

- The MVP should keep one visible tradeoff: low-cost/high-carbon coal versus higher-cost/cleaner options.
- It should keep one ventilation rule: blocking wind outlets with coal facilities reduces citywide ventilation.
- It should collect minimal feedback: the final layout, three metrics, and two survey answers.
